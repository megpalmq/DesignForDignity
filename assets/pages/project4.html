About the Project

As seniors age, managing chronic conditions like diabetes, arthritis, and digestive issues becomes essential. A balanced diet can ease symptoms and improve health. For diabetes, focus on meals low in refined sugars and high in fiber. For arthritis, include omega-3 fatty acids from fatty fish and flaxseeds to reduce inflammation. For acid reflux, opt for gentle, low-acid foods like oatmeal and bananas, and for constipation, include fiber-rich foods like whole grains, fruits, and vegetables. This brochure offers meal suggestions tailored to these needs, promoting healthier aging.

Design Process

  1. Research & Discovery: We started by researching the most common chronic conditions among seniors and how diet impacts their health. This helped us understand the essential nutritional needs and restrictions for individuals dealing with diabetes, arthritis, acid reflux, and constipation.
  2. Concept Development: After gathering insights, we curated meal suggestions that fit the dietary requirements for each condition. We aimed for easy-to-prepare, nutrient-dense meals that promote health and are accessible for seniors.
  3. Prototype Testing: The brochure uses a clean and organized layout to ensure clarity and ease of reading for older adults. We focused on large, legible fonts, high-contrast colors, and intuitive icons to guide the reader. Illustrations of healthy foods and seniors enjoying meals were included to make the brochure more engaging.
  4. Final Solution: Understanding that seniors may face difficulty with cooking or meal preparation, we included simple recipes and meal prep

Collaboration with Yonsei University

This project was a collaborative effort between Herron School of Art + Design and Yonsei University in Seoul, South Korea. Together, we researched food insecurity in both cities and developed solutions to address the challenges faced by vulnerable populations. By leveraging our shared expertise, we created a design that was culturally relevant and adaptable to the needs of both communities.

Throughout this collaboration, our teams engaged in constant communication, exchanging ideas and feedback to ensure the project was culturally sensitive and impactful. Yonsei University provided valuable insights into the local needs of their community, allowing us to tailor the design to the unique challenges faced by residents in both Songdo and Seoul. This exchange of knowledge helped us develop a more comprehensive solution that bridges the gap between two very different but equally important communities.
