Indiana

Food insecurity is a pressing issue affecting many residents of Indiana, particularly in rural and urban areas. Despite the state's agricultural wealth, a significant number of Hoosiers face challenges in accessing nutritious, affordable food.

This issue is often exacerbated by high poverty rates, unemployment, and a lack of access to food retailers in certain regions, resulting in food deserts. The elderly population is especially vulnerable, with many facing difficulties in obtaining fresh produce, largely due to limited mobility and fixed incomes. In cities like Indianapolis, Fort Wayne, and Evansville, low-income households struggle to meet basic nutritional needs, leading to poor health outcomes and increased reliance on food pantries and community food banks.

Furthermore, food insecurity in Indiana has a significant ripple effect, impacting children's education, physical and mental development, and contributing to the cycle of poverty.

Boner Center for Civic Engagement

The Boner Center for Civic Engagement in Indianapolis plays a vital role in combating food insecurity in the local community. Through its initiatives, the center helps provide access to healthy and nutritious food for residents facing challenges, especially in underserved areas and food deserts. The center collaborates with local food banks, nonprofits, and community organizations to address the root causes of food insecurity, focusing on long-term solutions that empower individuals and families.


The center also provides educational programs and volunteer opportunities that engage students and community members in efforts to support food access and nutrition. By fostering collaboration and community-driven solutions, the Bonner Center is helping to reduce food insecurity and improve the quality of life for many Indianapolis residents.

About the Collaboration

Yonsei University and Herron School of Art + Design students united to create impactful designs addressing food scarcity and health challenges for elderly communities in Korea and Indianapolis. Each team was assigned a distinct topic and presentation style, using visuals, typography, and layout to communicate the message effectively.

This collaboration was a valuable opportunity for students to learn from one another, explore diverse design techniques, and gain hands-on experience in addressing critical social issues through creative storytelling.
