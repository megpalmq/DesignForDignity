In the U.S., food insecurity disproportionately affects older adults, with Indiana experiencing higher rates than the national average.

A design study in Indianapolis focused on the unique challenges faced by older adults, such as dietary needs, food deserts, mobility and accessibility issues, and the impact of social services on their mental and social well-being. The research aims to identify effective changes in food systems and support structures to improve the overall quality of life for older adults facing these difficulties.
