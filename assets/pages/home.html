Food insecurity is a growing global crisis, affecting millions of people who struggle to access nutritious and sufficient food. From urban food deserts to rural hunger, economic disparities, climate change, and conflict continue to exacerbate the issue. Design for Dignity aims to shed light on these challenges through thoughtful design, raising awareness and fostering solutions that promote food accessibility, sustainability, and dignity for all.


Empathy  Driven Designers.

Designers play a crucial role in addressing complex challenges like food insecurity through socio-cultural reimagining and collaboration with stakeholders. This study shifts from a deficiency-based approach, focusing on creative ways to communicate the link between food access and mental well-being. It explores design solutions to improve communication about food security for the elderly in Indianapolis.

A key part of the project involved a pedagogical approach within the Visual Communication Design curriculum, developed through a virtual exchange with students from South Korea. The results will be shared in a poster that highlights community engagement, design research methods, and frameworks for evaluating the impact on the community.
